Marine Biology Club

Aim

The purpose of our club is to spread awareness about current welfare of our marine ecosystem. It is currently under attack, and by educating the Maria Carrillo student population, we hope to make an impact.

Each puma should understand their role and the impact they can have on the ocean. The importance of staying up to date on current legislative matters detailing the Marine Ecosystem is extremely important. Through this club we hope each member gains a more comprehensive understanding about the action they can take to make their voice heard

Opinion Column


Japan's decision to resume commercial whaling is stupid. Plain and simple. Yes, whaling may be a tradition in Japanese culture, but the thing about traditions is that they change. Whalers are estatic and jumping at the chance after hearing about this ban. Yoshifumi Kai, head of the Japan Small-Type Whaling Association "wants more people to try to taste [whale]," but at what cost does this come at? While the species of whales that Japananese whalers are after--Minke and Bryde--are not endangered, it is clear that this ban is the first step towards opening the flood gates. Because their argument is that whaling is apart of their culture, which is understandable, times have changed and our environment cannot sustain this tradition.
